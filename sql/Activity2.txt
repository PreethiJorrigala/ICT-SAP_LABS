Exercise 2:
Atomicity: 
	Transaction is either completed or rollback
	Example: During an online money transfer, if money is deducted from your account but not added to the receiver, the transaction is cancelled and your money is returned when you make a transaction .

Consistency:
	The database must remain correct before and after the transaction.
	Example: If a bank account must not go below ₹0, the system will stop a withdrawal that breaks this rule.

Isolation:
	Multiple transactions do not affect each other while running.
	Example: Two people booking the last train seat at the same time — only one booking succeeds.

Durability:
	Once a transaction is completed, it is permanently saved even after power failure.
	Example: After payment on a shopping site, the order remains recorded even if the system crashes.
